Helane Levine-Keating is a photographer, writer, and educator.

Levine-Keating’s photographic images evolve symbiotically with her creative writing, with each functioning as muse for the other. Her themes include rural landscapes, underwater Medusae (jellies) and sea anemones.

As a professor of modern and contemporary Comparative Literature and Creative Writing at Pace University since 1983, she incorporates art, photography, and film in her courses. She is also the co-editor of three editions of Lives Through Literature: A Thematic Anthology, 3e, Prentice-Hall, 2001.
